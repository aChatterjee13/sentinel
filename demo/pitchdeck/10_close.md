# Slide 10 — The close

## The ask

> *"Can we run a 4-week pilot on one of your models?"*

## What a pilot looks like

| Week | What happens | Who's involved |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Config + integration, live Slack alerts | 1 ML engineer |
| 2 | Backfill historical window, show caught issues | ML + risk |
| 3 | First canary rollout through Sentinel | ML + platform |
| 4 | Compliance evidence pack + decision meeting | ML + risk + compliance |

## What we bring

- Sentinel SDK + all source
- A Zensar engineer on-site / on-call for the full 4 weeks
- A playbook matched to your regulatory environment
- A synthetic dataset for any test you want to run without risk

## What you bring

- One non-critical model (start small)
- One dev environment (Azure / AWS / on-prem — we don't care)
- 3 hours / week of your ML + compliance leads' time
- One tough question from your auditor

## The decision point

At the end of week 4, you either:

1. **Expand**: we move to 5–10 models and plan the full rollout, or
2. **Walk away**: the pilot work is yours to keep. No contract tail.
